Financial Projections Tool

Single-file HTML tool for South African business-plan projections. Edit assumptions, edit output tables, switch between monthly and annual periods, generate visual reports, and export formatted Excel outputs.

Business Setup

This tab defines the base structure of the model. These settings control the reporting horizon, business profile, currency, tax and VAT assumptions, and working-capital timing used throughout the projections.

Vision

Narrative inputs used when copying projections into a business plan. These are not used in calculations but are kept inside the same project file for export and planning consistency.

How the model works

The engine builds revenue, payroll, operating expenses, loan schedules, depreciation and working capital to produce integrated statements. Output values can also be edited directly on the Statements tab when you want manual adjustments for business-plan presentation.

Financial Statements

This tab presents the projected Income Statement, Cash Flow Statement and Balance Sheet. Values are pre-filled from the model but can also be edited directly for presentation purposes. Recalculate rebuilds everything from the inputs again.

Editable outputs are useful when you need final polishing for a business plan. Direct edits are included in exports and now flow across linked statements.

Projected Profit and Loss Report (Income Statement)

Shows profitability over the selected period. It tracks revenue, cost of sales, gross profit, operating expenses, EBITDA, depreciation, finance cost, tax and net profit.

Projected Cash Flow Statement

Shows where cash comes from and where it goes. It combines operating cash flow, investing activity and financing activity to show cash movement and closing cash.

Projected Balance Sheet

Shows assets, liabilities and equity at each period-end. It reflects working capital, cash balances, debt outstanding, fixed assets and retained earnings.
